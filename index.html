01 — Research Interests

What Drives My Research

🔬

Mechanistic Interpretability

Understanding the internal circuits of LLMs — how knowledge is stored, retrieved, and composed. Working with tools like Logit Lens, PatchScopes, Logit Flow, and Edge Attribution Patching (EAP) to reverse-engineer multi-hop reasoning.

✏️

Knowledge Editing

Precisely modifying factual knowledge within LLMs without full retraining. Studying ROME, MEMIT, CaKE, and circuit-aware editing methods that respect the model's internal knowledge circuits.

🧠

Multi-hop Reasoning

Investigating how LLMs chain multiple facts together for complex inference. Analyzing circuit-level failure modes and developing editing techniques that preserve reasoning integrity.

📊

Time-Series Anomaly Detection

Applying machine learning to real-time monitoring data. Built anomaly detection pipelines integrating Prometheus metrics with deep learning models for system health monitoring.

🤖

LLMs for Applications

Building practical AI systems — from CodeMate, an LLM-powered coding assistant, to exploring LLM deployment architectures with Azure AI Foundry and open-source models.

🎯

Out-of-Distribution Detection

Improving model robustness by detecting inputs that fall outside the training distribution — a foundational concern for deploying reliable AI in the real world.
